Assessment explanation

Task

Assignment 1 focused on understanding the marketing mix underpinning an existing product and explored how you undertook decision-making and your in-store customer experience when selecting that product. In Assignment 2, you're taking on the role of being the marketing manager within a new, small start-up. You've been tasked with producing a report for the start-up's founder that explores how you would bring the type of product you examined in Assignment 1 to consumers via the start-up. You’ll put together a report for the start-up's founder which aims to understand the current marketing environment and the potential market segments which will inform the targeting and positioning of the product.

***Important**: You’re now viewing the product from Assignment 1 at the** product category** level in this assignment. Let’s say you selected Nike Air Max sneakers in Assignment 1, in this assignment you’re just interested in the product category of ‘sneakers’.*

Instructions

**Cover Page **

At the front of your assignment, there should be a cover page. This clear and professional cover page should include your name, student number, unit code and title, teacher name, and assessment title. If you have used GenAI, include your statement on how it was used here (see our [<u>Use of Generative AI</u>](https://swinburne.instructure.com/courses/75910/pages/gea66d9059401cc0479d9c4435901acae) page for more information). 

**Table of Contents**

After the cover page, include a table of contents on a separate page which lists the main headings, or any sub-headings, within the assignment. The table of contents should be on a page by itself. The written assignment then begins on the following page.

**Introduction ***(approx. 50 - 100 words)*

Write a short introduction to your report. Your introduction should state the product category which forms the focus of the report (e.g.
'sneakers'), explain the purpose of the report (why it is being written), who it is being written for (the start-up's founder) and
include a brief overview of the key areas of the report (what will be covered). 

**Section 1** **– The Marketing Environment ***(approx. 500 - 700 words)*

***Microenvironment analysis ***

Select **one** microenvironmental factor from the following list and briefly explain it using a reference.

- Suppliers
- Intermediaries
- Publics

For your chosen microenvironmental factor:

- Explain why your chosen microenvironmental factor is important for the start‑up to consider.
- Describe one way in which the microenvironmental factor could potentially **positively impact** customer perceived value towards the start-up's product.
- Describe one way in which the microenvironmental factor could potentially **negatively impact** customer perceived value towards the start-up's product.

  - *You might consider how or what materials might be sourced, how the product is made available or promoted etc).*

 

***Macroenvironment analysis ***

Select **one** macroenvironmental force and briefly explain it using a
reference.

- Demographic forces
- Economic forces
- Natural forces
- Technological forces
- Political forces
- Cultural forces

For your chosen macroenvironmental force:

- Identify one current trend relating to this factor which could impact the start-up, using an appropriate reference to support that this trend exists. 

  - *For example, if you selected ‘technological forces’. you could explore how a trend relating to this factor (e.g. increasing accessibility of artificial intelligence) might be impacting the general business environment you’d be operating in, or perhaps how it might impact the specific product being sold by the start-up.*

  - *See the resources section at the bottom of the task description for links to databases you could potentially use to identify a relevant trend.*

- Describe one opportunity created by this trend and how this could **positively impact** the start-up.

- Describe one threat created by the trend and how this could** negatively impact **the start-up.

 

**Section 2 – Marketing Research ***(approx. 300 - 400 words)*

In this section, you will explain how the start‑up would conduct market research on consumers who currently use similar products.

- Select** one** primary or secondary data collection method that would be valuable to help you learn about how consumers currently use similar products in the market.

  - **Primary data methods**: physical or online surveys, interviews, observations etc.

  - **Secondary data methods**: government sources, news articles, academic journal articles, industry reports, market research reports, syndicated sources etc.

<!-- -->

- Explain the value of using your selected data collection method (e.g. interview), what could be learned about their consumer behaviour through this approach, and how this understanding might benefit the start-up.

  - *For example, why is holding an interview with a consumer useful, what could be learned about how or why they use* *similar products, and how could this could information be useful to inform the creation or marketing of the start-up’s product?*

<!-- -->

- Explain how you would implement this data collection method to best understand how consumers currently use similar products. Justify why your proposed approach is appropriate.

  - *If you’ve selected a primary data method such as an interview, where would you recruit consumers of similar products to take part in an interview? Why would you select this location/s or online environment/s?*

  - *If you’ve selected a secondary data approach, which websites, data sources or platforms, social media pages, or journals/magazines/newspapers etc would provide useful insights into consumers of similar products? Why would you use this source/s?*

 

**Section 3 – Segmentation ***(approx. 300 - 500 words)*

In this section, you will identify appropriate target market segments for the start‑up's product.

- Explain what market segmentation is and its value for marketers, using a reference.

- Select **at least one** segmentation variable that the start‑up should use to define its target market (e.g. demographic, geographic, psychographic, behavioural). 

- Identify the key market segments within each segmentation variable you have selected, which you believe would be valuable for the start-up to target.

  - *For example, if you selected two segmentation variables: **Demographic**: age: 18 – 30, **Geographic**: urban locations in Australia.*

- Justify why these key market segments are appropriate for the start‑up to target, considering factors such as whether they are **measurable**, **accessible**, **substantial**, **differentiable, **or **actionable**.

  - *You do not need to cover all these factors, just those which are most relevant to your segmentation variable/s.*

  - *You should use references to support your justification where possible.*

***Reminder**: You’re allowed to draw from your own personal characteristics to inform the key market segment (i.e. “I like sneakers, and I know others in my age group do too, so I think my age group fits”). However, try and use a reference to support that this market segment would be ideal to target. Do not reference yourself within your justification.*

 

**Section 4 – Positioning ***(approx. 300 - 400 words)*

Now that you’ve identified key market segments, you’ll now explore how the start-up could position their product relative to competitors.

- Identify 2 – 4 competitors within the product category who sell a similar product

  - *For example, if your product category is ‘sneakers’, competitors could be Nike Air Max and Adidas Ultraboost.*

- Position the competitors within a value proposition positioning map. Write out the brand and their competing product’s name in the relevant section. Images can also be used (optional). **[<u>Here is a template of the positioning map that you can use in your assignment</u>](https://swinburne.instructure.com/courses/75910/files/43505098?wrap=1)[ Download Here is a template of the positioning map that you can use in your assignment](https://swinburne.instructure.com/courses/75910/files/43505098/download?download_frd=1).**

<!-- -->

- Justify why you’ve categorised the competitors as such, based on any benefits provided by a competitor’s product relative to how it’s priced.

- Based on any gaps or opportunities you see, **recommend** a value proposition category which the start-up could position their product in. Explain why this could be an effective position given the competitors’ positioning and what you know about your target market segment/s.

 

**Conclusion ***(approx. 100 words)*

Summarise the key insights of your analysis. Your conclusion should summarise each section of the assignment. One sentence for each section is recommended:

- Insights from your microenvironment and macroenvironment analysis (Section 1)

- What data collection approach you believe would uncover consumer insights, and how these insights could potentially benefit the start-up (Section 2).

- A clear statement of your key market segments and why they are valuable for the start-up to target (Section 3).

- Insights from your positioning analysis which inform the recommended positioning for the start-up (Section 4).

Keep the conclusion brief, it is mostly a top-level summary of the main findings of each section of the report. There is no need to add new information here.

 

**Appendix (optional)**

Include any information here which you would like to include which supplements aspects of your assignment. Note that you only should put information in the appendix if it doesn't directly add to the main body of the assignment, it merely is a 'nice to know'.
