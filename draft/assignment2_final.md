## Introduction

This report proposes a market entry strategy for G'MORNIN Rise & Shine black tea bags (pack format: 25 × 2 g = 50 g box) entering the Vietnamese market. Written for the start-up's founder, the purpose of this report is to analyze the current marketing environment and potential consumer segments to inform a targeted positioning strategy. The report begins with a micro- and macroenvironment analysis (Section 1), followed by a proposed primary market research methodology (Section 2). It then establishes a target market profile (Section 3), details competitor positioning (Section 4), and concludes with a summary of strategic recommendations.

---

## Section 1 – The Marketing Environment

### Microenvironment Analysis: Suppliers

The microenvironment consists of actors close to the company that affect its ability to serve customers, and suppliers are critical actors that provide the resources needed by the company to produce its goods and services (Kotler & Armstrong, 2020). For a start-up like G'MORNIN, whose core differentiator is a sustainable black tea bag, supplier management is not merely an operational detail; it is the foundation of the brand's value proposition. Sourcing certified compostable filter media (such as PLA-based or abaca-fiber filter materials) is essential to support the brand's primary marketing claims.

Sourcing certified compostable filter media from Ahlstrom (InFuse™/BioWeb®) or Nonwoven Network (One Earth®) allows G'MORNIN to carry eco-labels (BPI/OK Compost). In Vietnam, eco-labeling and packaging credibility significantly enhance consumer trust, driving perceived value and purchase intentions (Nguyen et al., 2021; Nhu-Ty Nguyen et al., 2021). This is supported by findings that packaging quality and perceived environmental effectiveness exert the strongest positive impacts on urban Vietnamese purchase intentions (Lan et al., 2023). Sourcing premium black tea leaves ensures taste consistency, reinforcing quality-led value.

Conversely, partnering with unverified suppliers who make unsubstantiated biodegradable claims leads to greenwashing accusations. Sceptical urban consumers will reject the brand, destroying credibility. Additionally, sourcing uncertified synthetic filters can cause microplastic or chemical leaching during brewing, destroying the health-focused perceived value and driving customers away (Nguyen-Viet et al., 2023).

### Macroenvironment Analysis: Cultural Forces

Cultural forces shape values, perceptions, and behaviors (Kotler & Armstrong, 2020). A major shift in urban Vietnam is the rise of green consumerism among young, educated demographics (Nhu-Ty Nguyen et al., 2021). This cultural force is reflected in sustainable packaging preferences; urban Vietnamese consumers exhibit positive attitudes and willingness to pay (WTP) a premium for products reducing plastic waste, provided claims are clear and functional (Nguyen Le et al., 2026).

This creates an opportunity for G'MORNIN. In Vietnam's F&B industry, green product attributes, green distribution, and green pricing are the most critical drivers of green purchase decisions (Cao Tuan Khanh et al., 2026). Framing the compostable filter as a practical morning routine choice builds brand equity, linking sustainability with a daily habit rather than a niche lifestyle (Nguyen-Viet et al., 2023).

However, this trend also brings a threat of consumer skepticism and greenwashing fatigue. If G'MORNIN makes environmental claims without visible third-party logos, skeptical buyers will reject the brand. In Vietnam, lack of transparency and mistrust in packaging claims are major barriers preventing green attitudes from translating into actual purchases (Nguyen et al., 2021).

## Section 2 – Marketing Research

To understand how Vietnamese consumers use similar packaged tea bag products, G'MORNIN should conduct primary qualitative research using **semi-structured in-depth interviews**. In-depth interviews probe a single respondent to uncover underlying motivations, beliefs, attitudes, and feelings (Malhotra et al., 2017). Qualitative interviews are highly effective in consumer research because they explore complex personal rituals and subjective meanings that quantitative surveys miss (Belk et al., 2013). This qualitative approach aligns with recent studies in Vietnam that used focus groups to capture consumer attitudes toward green-packaged beverages, revealing how social influences shape individual buying behavior (Cheng Lung Li et al., 2022).

The value of this method lies in its ability to uncover the "why" behind consumer behavior. Unlike surveys, which collect superficial data, in-depth interviews will reveal the specific morning rituals of urban professionals, their sensory preferences (such as flavor strength, bitterness, and the use of milk/condensed milk), and their awareness of plastic content in traditional tea bag filters. Furthermore, this method will explore consumer perceptions of eco-labels and their willingness to pay for biodegradable alternatives. This understanding benefits the start-up by identifying key product parameters (e.g., optimal bag shape, infusion speed) and refining promotional messaging to ensure it is credible and resonates with the target audience.

G'MORNIN will implement this method by conducting 20 semi-structured online interviews (using platforms like Zoom or MS Teams) with urban Vietnamese adults aged 22-40 who drink packaged tea bags at least four times a week. Participants will be recruited via targeted social media posts in local lifestyle and sustainability groups in Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi. Additionally, we will place digital poster boards with a QR code in the lobbies of major commercial office towers and coworking spaces (e.g., Dreamplex, Toong). Online interviews are appropriate as they respect the busy schedules of working office professionals. Recruiting from urban office hubs is critical since consumer awareness, reference groups, and environmental responsibility strongly drive the transition to green consumption in major Vietnamese cities (Nguyen, D. D., 2023). Participants will receive a VND 100,000 digital voucher to secure high-quality engagement.

## Section 3 – Segmentation

### Grounding Theory and Variables

Market segmentation divides a market into distinct groups of buyers with different needs, characteristics, or behaviors who require separate marketing mixes (Kotler & Armstrong, 2020). For a start-up, segmentation is vital to concentrate limited marketing resources on high-potential consumer groups, maximizing return on investment (Proctor, 2020). Effective segmentation helps new entrants bypass established competitors by targeting underserved niche markets with tailored offerings rather than attempting to serve the entire market (Dibb & Simkin, 2008). For G'MORNIN, we segment the Vietnamese packaged tea market using demographic, psychographic, and behavioural variables:

* **Demographic:** Young to middle-aged urban adults (aged 22–40) living in Tier 1 cities (Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi), who are university-educated office professionals with moderate-to-high disposable incomes.
* **Psychographic:** Health-conscious individuals, environmentally aware consumers (green routine builders), and busy professionals who value structured morning routines.
* **Behavioural:** Habitual black tea drinkers (consuming 1-2 cups daily), convenience seekers (preferring tea bags over loose-leaf), and active e-commerce (Shopee/Tiki) shoppers. In Vietnam, young F&B consumers' green purchase intentions are strongly driven by green perceived value, environmental knowledge, and digital marketing (Nguyen Phuc Hung et al., 2025).

### Target Segment Selection and MASDA Justification

Based on this segmentation, G'MORNIN's primary target market is the **Urban Eco-Professional** segment (office workers aged 22–40 who drink morning tea daily and prefer eco-friendly products). This segment is highly appropriate to target under the **MASDA** criteria (Kotler & Armstrong, 2020):

* **Measurable:** The segment's size and purchasing power are measurable. Market databases and urban census data provide clear statistics on the number of urban professionals in Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi, and their average monthly beverage expenditure.
* **Accessible:** The segment is accessible via digital media (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok) and modern-trade outlets like e-commerce (Shopee, Tiki), convenience stores (Circle K, GS25), and supermarkets (WinMart, Co.opmart), allowing direct, targeted promotions.
* **Substantial:** Sizable and profitable. Vietnam's tea market is valued at **USD 106.97 million in 2025** (IMARC Group, 2025). The high concentration of middle-class office workers in Hanoi and HCMC represents a substantial, high-volume group. Sourcing eco-packaging is also supported by green research indicating that corporate environmental responsibility strongly drives green purchase intentions in Vietnam, especially among female consumers (Vu et al., 2021).
* **Differentiable:** The segment is distinguishable as they respond uniquely to eco-friendly packaging and convenience, unlike traditional tea drinkers who prefer loose-leaf green tea and prioritize low cost over sustainability.
* **Actionable:** The segment is actionable; G'MORNIN can design a tailored marketing mix—including a 25-count box, online subscription discounts, and eco-educational digital campaigns—that directly addresses their unique needs.
* *References:* Targeting this segment is strongly supported by research showing that young, urban Vietnamese professionals are highly receptive to eco-packaging and are willing to pay a premium when brand trust and packaging clarity are established (Nguyen et al., 2021; Nhu-Ty Nguyen et al., 2021).

## Section 4 – Positioning

### Competitor Analysis

To determine how G'MORNIN should position its product, we analyze four major competitors in the Vietnamese packaged tea bag market:

1. **Trà Lài Savo** (VND 31,680): Value Anchor. A jasmine green tea bag listing that targets price-sensitive consumers, competing on low cost and convenience. It uses non-recyclable packaging, offering no environmental value.
2. **Trà Đen Cozy** (VND 49,000): Mid-Market Local. A popular domestic black tea brand offering mainstream quality and convenience, but with standard packaging and no sustainability claims.
3. **Trà Lài Phúc Long** (VND 48,000–59,000): Mass-Market Leader. Phúc Long's jasmine green tea bags own scale and high familiarity, representing a quality mainstream benchmark, but use standard non-recyclable filters.
4. **Twinings Earl Grey** (VND 172,000): Premium Imported. Sells at a high premium based on heritage and quality, but does not promote local packaging sustainability in Vietnam.

### Justification and Recommended Position

Savo sits in the lower-left quadrant due to low pricing and basic packaging. Cozy and Phúc Long sit in the mid-left, offering local quality with low sustainability credibility. Twinings (VND 172,000) and Dilmah (VND 90,000) represent high-price imported anchors but lack local sustainable packaging initiatives. 

We recommend positioning G'MORNIN in the **mid-price, high-sustainability** space (VND 55,000–60,000). A firm's green brand positioning directly influences green brand attitudes and purchase intentions, with willingness-to-pay acting as a moderator (Mehraj et al., 2022). In Vietnam, packaging design elements (graphics, color, shape) significantly drive brand trust and tea-bag purchase intentions (Khuong & Nguyen, 2018), and packaging design strongly influences FMCG purchasing decisions in Ho Chi Minh City (Tri Dang Quan et al., 2021).

This position is highly effective because it:
1. Exploits a clear market gap, as no competitor offers a certified compostable tea bag at a mainstream price.
2. Aligns with our target's WTP a 10–20% premium over Cozy/Phúc Long, remaining below expensive imported brands (Nguyen Le et al., 2026). Compostable filters satisfy both green intrinsic and extrinsic motivations, which jointly drive actual green consumption in Vietnam (Duong, C. D., 2023).
3. Uses certifications (Ahlstrom/Nonwoven Network) to build green trust and prevent greenwashing skepticism (Nguyen-Viet et al., 2023).
